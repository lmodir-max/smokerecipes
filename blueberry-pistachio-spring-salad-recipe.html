A Note From My Kitchen

The biggest difference between a “meh” salad and a crave-worthy one is dry greens. After rinsing the baby spinach, take an extra moment to pat it really dry so the dressing clings instead of pooling at the bottom. If your spinach is even a little wet, the salad can taste watered down fast.

When you add the blueberries, treat them gently—think of it like folding a delicate batter. If they get crushed, they’ll streak the bowl and soften the spinach. Keeping them whole gives you those juicy bursts that make every bite feel fresh.

If red onion ever feels too sharp for you, you can slice it extra thin so it blends in more subtly. The onion should add flavor and a little crunch, not overpower the blueberries and feta.

Finally, dress right before serving if you want maximum crispness. The balsamic vinaigrette is delicious, but like any dressing, it will soften spinach over time. A gentle toss is all you need—just enough to lightly coat without bruising the leaves.

Ingredient Notes and Easy Swaps:

This salad is simple, but each ingredient plays a specific role in the final flavor and texture. Think of it as a balance of sweet, salty, tangy, and crunchy—built on a fresh spinach base. Use the freshest blueberries you can for the brightest flavor and the prettiest presentation. Pistachios add that roasted nuttiness that makes the salad feel hearty even though it’s no-cook. And the dressing is the “glue” that brings everything together, so add it gradually and toss gently.

  • Baby spinach: This is your tender, mild base that lets the toppings shine. Make sure it’s well-dried so it stays perky and doesn’t dilute the dressing.
  • Fresh blueberries: They bring sweet, juicy pops that feel especially “spring.” Choose berries that are plump and firm; softer berries are more likely to burst when folded in.
  • Roasted unsalted pistachios: These add crunch and a toasty, savory edge. Roughly chopping gives you a mix of sizes so you get crunch in every bite without overpowering the salad.
  • Crumbled feta cheese: Feta adds creamy saltiness that balances the fruit. If your feta is very moist, crumble it right over the bowl so it stays fluffy and doesn’t clump.
  • Thinly sliced red onion: Onion brings bite and a little crisp snap. Slicing thin is key here—too thick and it can dominate the delicate spinach and blueberries.
  • Balsamic vinaigrette: This provides tangy sweetness and a glossy finish. Drizzle it on gradually so you can stop when everything looks lightly coated, not drenched.
  • Texture balance tip: This salad is at its best when the pistachios stay crunchy. If you’re prepping ahead, keep the nuts separate until the last moment so they don’t soften.
  • Flavor balance tip: The combination of feta and balsamic vinaigrette is naturally bold. If you prefer a softer flavor, toss very gently and aim for a light coating rather than a heavy drizzle.
Tool / Substitution Note: A large mixing bowl makes gentle folding easier and helps keep the blueberries intact. If you don’t have one, use any wide bowl so you can toss lightly without crushing the fruit.

How to Make Blueberry Pistachio Spring Salad (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Give the baby spinach a quick rinse under cold water, then pat it thoroughly dry so it stays crisp. Add it to a large mixing bowl with plenty of room for tossing later.

Step 2: Add the fresh blueberries and fold them in gently so they stay whole and juicy. You’re aiming for blueberries nestled throughout the greens, not smashed into them.

Step 3: Scatter the roughly chopped roasted unsalted pistachios over the spinach and blueberries. The mix of small and slightly larger pieces helps distribute crunch evenly.

Step 4: Sprinkle the crumbled feta over the top so it lands in little creamy pockets. This gives you salty, tangy bites that balance the sweetness of the berries.

Step 5: Add the thinly sliced red onion, separating the slices so they don’t clump together. You want a light, even scatter for flavor in every forkful.

Step 6: Drizzle the balsamic vinaigrette over the salad and toss very gently until everything looks lightly coated. Stop as soon as the spinach is glossy and the toppings are evenly distributed to keep the leaves from wilting.

Optional Icing:

Optional (and not traditional for salad): whisk powdered sugar with a tiny splash of milk until smooth, then drizzle very lightly over the finished salad for a sweet contrast. Use sparingly so it doesn’t mask the balsamic vinaigrette.

Tips For Success:

  • Dry the spinach well so the balsamic vinaigrette clings instead of watering down the bowl.
  • Fold blueberries in gently to keep them intact and prevent purple streaking.
  • Add dressing gradually and toss lightly—spinach can wilt quickly once coated.
  • Slice red onion very thin so it adds bite without overpowering the feta and fruit.
  • For the best crunch, keep pistachios dry and add them right before serving.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Can I make this salad ahead of time?

You can prep the components ahead, but wait to add the balsamic vinaigrette until just before serving to keep the spinach crisp. For best texture, add pistachios at the last minute too.

How do I keep the blueberries from breaking?

Use firm, fresh blueberries and fold them in gently rather than tossing aggressively. A larger bowl helps because the ingredients have space to move without getting crushed.

What’s the best way to serve it for a light lunch?

Serve it immediately after tossing so the greens stay perky and the pistachios stay crunchy. Portion into four bowls for the intended serving size and enjoy while it’s fresh and glossy.
