A Note From My Kitchen

This is one of those bakes that looks extra special without being complicated. The batter should be mixed only until the dry ingredients disappear. If you keep going, the cake can lose some of that soft, tender texture that makes it so appealing.

The cinnamon swirl mixture should feel creamy and spreadable, not stiff or dry. When you drop it over the batter, try to distribute it in little spoonfuls across the pan so every slice gets some of that buttery cinnamon flavor.

When swirling, less is more. A few gentle passes with a butter knife create those beautiful ribbons without muddying the batter. You want distinct swirls, not a fully blended top.

If you like an extra gooey finish, the recipe notes mention pouring heavy cream over the batter before baking. You can also use the optional pinch of salt or a little cream cheese in the glaze for a more balanced, slightly tangy finish.

Ingredient Notes and Easy Swaps:

This cake uses simple baking staples, but each ingredient plays an important role in the final texture and flavor. The base is soft and rich, while the swirl adds that signature cinnamon roll character. The glaze ties everything together and gives the top that classic bakery-style finish. A few optional changes can personalize the recipe without changing the core method. As always, keep the main structure the same for the best results. These notes will help you understand what each ingredient is doing in the pan.

  • All-purpose flour: This gives the cake its structure and keeps the crumb soft but sturdy enough to hold the swirl. For a gluten-free version, the recipe notes suggest a 1:1 gluten-free flour blend, and it is important to make sure the baking powder is also gluten-free.
  • Granulated sugar: This sweetens the cake batter and helps create a tender texture. It is best to keep it as written in the batter so the crumb stays balanced and moist.
  • Baking powder: Since this is not a yeast-based recipe, baking powder provides the lift. Make sure it is fresh so the cake rises evenly and does not bake up dense.
  • Salt: A small amount sharpens all the sweet and buttery flavors. If you enjoy a slightly more balanced glaze, the optional pinch of salt there is a nice touch too.
  • Milk: Milk adds moisture and helps create a smooth batter. The glaze also uses milk, and you can adjust the amount there to make it thinner or thicker depending on how you like it to drizzle.
  • Eggs: Eggs help bind the batter and support the cake as it bakes. They also add richness, which works beautifully with the vanilla and butter.
  • Vanilla extract: Vanilla gives warmth to both the cake and the glaze. It rounds out the sweetness and makes the whole pan smell incredible as it cools.
  • Unsalted butter: Melted butter enriches the batter, while softened butter creates the swirl mixture. Using unsalted butter keeps the sweetness and spice in balance.
  • Light brown sugar: This is what gives the swirl that classic caramel-like depth. Packed brown sugar also helps the cinnamon layer stay soft and gooey after baking.
  • Ground cinnamon: Cinnamon is the heart of the flavor here, turning a simple cake into a true cinnamon breakfast cake. If you want extra texture, the notes mention adding chopped pecans or walnuts to the swirl.
  • Powdered sugar: This forms the smooth, sweet glaze that melts into the warm cake. It is what gives the top that familiar finish you expect from a cinnamon roll-inspired bake.
  • Optional add-ins: The notes suggest chopped apples, raisins, or cream cheese for variation. Those additions can shift the flavor profile a bit while still keeping the spirit of the recipe intact.
A 9×13-inch baking dish is ideal for the full recipe, and an 8×8-inch pan works if you cut the recipe in half as noted. If you plan to freeze it, do so before glazing for the freshest texture when reheated.

How to Make Easy Cinnabon Cinnamon Roll Cake (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Start by heating your oven to 350°F and greasing a 9×13-inch baking dish well. Make sure the corners and sides are coated so the cake releases easily after baking.

Step 2: In a large bowl, combine the flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt. Give everything a thorough whisk so the leavening is evenly distributed before the wet ingredients go in.

Step 3: Add the milk, eggs, vanilla extract, and melted butter to the bowl. Mix just until the batter comes together and looks smooth with no dry streaks, being careful not to overwork it.

Step 4: Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and spread it into an even layer. It will be thick enough to hold the swirl on top without sinking too quickly.

Step 5: In a separate bowl, cream together the softened butter, brown sugar, flour, and cinnamon. The mixture should look rich, thick, and spreadable, almost like a soft cinnamon filling.

Step 6: Drop spoonfuls of the cinnamon mixture all over the batter, then gently drag a butter knife through the top to create swirls. A few light passes are enough to marble the cake without blending everything together.

Step 7: Bake for 35 to 40 minutes, until the top is lightly golden and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out mostly clean. A little cinnamon mixture on the toothpick is perfectly fine and expected.

Step 8: Let the cake rest for 10 to 15 minutes after it comes out of the oven. This short cooling time helps the crumb settle while keeping the cake warm enough for the glaze to soak in beautifully.

Step 9: Whisk together the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth. Add a little more or less milk as needed until the glaze reaches a pourable consistency that drizzles easily.

Step 10: Drizzle the glaze evenly over the warm cake and let it sit for a moment before slicing. The glaze will settle into the swirls and create that signature sweet finish.

Optional Icing:

If you want an extra layer of sweetness on the side, you can stir together powdered sugar, a little milk, and a drop of vanilla to make a quick additional icing. A tiny pinch of salt or a small splash of cream cheese can make it taste richer and more balanced. This is completely optional and separate from the main glaze in the recipe.

Tips For Success:

  • Mix the batter only until combined so the cake stays soft and tender instead of turning heavy.
  • Space the cinnamon mixture across the pan before swirling so every slice gets plenty of buttery flavor.
  • Do not over-swirl the top; distinct ribbons look prettier and give a more pronounced cinnamon roll taste.
  • Glaze the cake while it is still warm so it lightly melts into the surface instead of sitting in a thick layer.
  • For reheating, microwave individual slices for 10 to 15 seconds to bring back that fresh-from-the-oven softness.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Can I make this ahead of time?

Yes, this cake holds up well for several days when covered at room temperature. You can also freeze it before glazing, then thaw and finish with glaze closer to serving time.

How do I know when the cake is done?

The top should look set and lightly golden, and a toothpick inserted into the center should come out mostly clean. A little cinnamon swirl on the toothpick is normal and does not mean the cake is underbaked.

Can I add mix-ins or variations?

Absolutely. The recipe notes suggest chopped apples, raisins, cream cheese, or even nuts in the swirl, all of which can add a fun twist while keeping the same cozy flavor profile.
